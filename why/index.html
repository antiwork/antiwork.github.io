Why do this?

Antiwork is a free "job" board for finding open source projects to work on.

The name comes from this article.
"Antiwork is what we do out of love, fun, interest, talent, enthusiasm, inspiration, etc. Only a lucky few get paid enough from it to live on, yet it probably enriches our lives and benefits society more than most jobs do."

For a potential contributor finding a new project can be a pain, fixing bugs isn't so enticing and issues aren't always orientated at those who aren't familiar. In all, open source software projects are never really sold to the newcomer.

For a project owner, the churn rate for contributors can be extreme often resulting in a precious few remaining to be the sole maintainers. The 'new blood' comes in the form of power users with single issue projects or fixes for minor irritations. A lone maintainer is never going to be capable of performing outreach on the level Mozilla can.

Antiwork is designed to cost as little as possible, hopefully zero, with as little maintence as possible. There is no proper backend here, just github pages and jekyll.
